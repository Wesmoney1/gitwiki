**Retrocomputing** is the current use of [older computer
hardware](Vintage_computer "older computer hardware"){.wikilink} and
[software](software "software"){.wikilink}. Retrocomputing is usually
classed as a [hobby](hobby "hobby"){.wikilink} and recreation rather
than a practical application of technology; enthusiasts often collect
rare and valuable [hardware](Electronic_hardware "hardware"){.wikilink}
and [software](software "software"){.wikilink} for sentimental
reasons.[^1]

Occasionally, however, an obsolete computer system has to be
\"resurrected\" to run software specific to that system, to access data
stored on [obsolete
media](Digital_obsolescence "obsolete media"){.wikilink}, or to use a
[peripheral](peripheral "peripheral"){.wikilink} that requires that
system. ![Retrosystem 2010, a retrocomputing event in
[Athens](Athens "Athens"){.wikilink}](Retrocomputing_2010_Athens_Greece.jpg "Retrosystem 2010, a retrocomputing event in Athens")

Retrocomputing and [retro
gaming](retro_gaming "retro gaming"){.wikilink} has been described as
[preservation](Digital_preservation "preservation"){.wikilink} activity
and as aspects of the [remix
culture](remix_culture "remix culture"){.wikilink}.[^2]
